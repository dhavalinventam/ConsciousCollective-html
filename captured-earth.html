‘Cap’tured Earth - A Climate Art Installation

by Greener Earth Foundation Artist Aakash Ranison

This sweeping ocean wave you see before you is 'Cap'tured Earth; made entirely from tens of thousands of discarded plastic bottle caps. Created by Greener Earth Foundation, the artist Aakash Ranison transforms one of the most littered single-use plastics into a warning we can no longer ignore.

Every cap in this wave represents a harsh truth: maximum of them – the caps – never make it to recycling. Waste pickers are paid by weight, so the bottle is collected, but the lightweight cap is left behind to drift into landfills, rivers, and eventually the sea. Globally, only 11% of all plastic ever produced is recycled. At this rate, by 2050 there may be more plastic in the ocean than fish.

But the Cap-tured Earth is not just a symbol of crisis, it is a reminder of possibility. Across the European Union, “tethered bottle caps” are now mandatory. With a small design change, the cap stays attached to the bottle, ensuring it gets recycled too; at no extra material, no extra cost, just better intent.

This installation asks us to imagine a world where every product is designed with the planet in mind, and where no piece of plastic is too small to matter.
