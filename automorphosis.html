AUTOmorphosis

By Anjala S S, National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad

0:00
1:42

What you see here began its life not in a studio, but on the road. AUTOmorphosis is crafted from automobile glass. A material engineered for safety, but nearly impossible to recycle. Laminated with plastic film, coated with ceramic frits, and dotted with impurities that protect us from heat and glare; windshield glass rarely makes it back into the recycling loop. Most scrapyards don't even take it. It becomes waste by default.

But, what if those impurities could become the design?

Through months of extraction, experimentation and material testing, Anjala – a student of NID, Ahemdabad, transformed discarded windshields into a translucent architectural tile. Every speck, tint and texture you see is not added but inherited.

Rather than mass-producing a new object, the work proposes small-scale, site-specific fabrication: tiles that interact with light, offer privacy, and can be used as panels or architectural inserts. And because they remain a mono-material, they can continue their journey – reused, re-formed, or recycled again.

AUTOmorphosis invites us to rethink value, and to see possibilities where the world only sees waste.
