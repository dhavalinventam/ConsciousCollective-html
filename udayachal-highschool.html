Welcome to Udayachal High School's Reclaiming the Cool section. A booth created by the young innovators at Udayachal High School. Here, the students explore how simple, traditional, and nature-based ideas can help cool our world gently, creatively, and sustainably.

Begin with the Earthen Fridge, inspired from the age-old cooling method that can be a powerful solution to today's rising temperature. This is a Zeer Pot made of two clay vessels and damp sand. As the moisture evaporates, it can naturally cools fruits and vegetables, needing no electricity!

Up next is the Natural Shade Umbrella, crafted from coir, dried leaves, and plant fibres. Unlike plastic umbrellas, these are made from breathable materials that allow air to flow freely, creating instant natural coolness beneath it. Fully biodegradable and low-cost, this umbrella shows how traditional textures and simple craftsmanship can offer comfort in rising heat

Now, you’ll find Rebus to Reclaim the Cool, a playful set of picture riddles placed alongside vetiver plants. Vetiver naturally cools and refreshes the air, while the rebus puzzles invites you to solve simple climate messages. This model blends learning with curiosity, showing how environmental awareness can grow through interactive and nature-based experiences.

And finally, we have the Rocking the Green Wave which uses a hand-built rocking mechanism attached to a traditional pankha fan. With every gentle motion, it creates a steady breeze without electricity. Made from simple materials, this model celebrates slow, handcrafted cooling methods and reminds us how human-powered solutions can reduce energy use

Together, these student-led ideas show that cooling the Earth does not always require technology. Sometimes, the answers are already in our hands — in clay, in bamboo, in plants, and in the imagination of the next generation.
