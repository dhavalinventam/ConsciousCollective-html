Reeweaving the ecosystem: Restoring India’s indigenous cotton legacy is The Cotton Story that invites you into the quiet, interwoven world of desi cotton. Desi cotton is India’s ancient, indigenous varieties that grow with almost no water, no chemicals, and no strain on the soil. These are seeds with a hundred years of memory. They once shaped entire village economies, but today they survive only in pockets, kept alive by farmers, spinners, and weavers who still work with their hands.

This installation brings together simple, pre-spinning and pre-weaving tools such as paddles, cards, charkhas, and other devices that were once found in every home. They are low-cost, easily repaired, and designed for community use. When cotton is processed by hand, every small adjustment changes the character of the yarn. No two threads are alike. That gentle unevenness, that signature touch, becomes the soul of the fabric.

You can also walk through a complete zero-waste circle, where every part of the plant has a purpose: fiber becomes cloth, seeds become oil, stems turn into rope and paper, and leaves return to the soil as compost.

This Cotton Story is a reminder that sustainability is not a trend, it is a practice of care, rooted in land, skill, and community. It shows us a future built not on mass production, but on knowledge, balance, and the wisdom of working with what we already have.
