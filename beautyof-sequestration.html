Welcome to The Beauty of Sequestration, a sculptural triptych that celebrates one of nature’s quietest climate heroes, fungi.

Fungi do far more than grow beneath our feet. They help cool the planet. As powerful decomposers, they break down fallen leaves, wood, and organic matter, transforming them into stable carbon stored deep within the soil for years. This process of carbon sequestration keeps enormous amounts of carbon dioxide from returning to the atmosphere.

Beneath the surface, fungi form vast mycorrhizal networks that connect plants like an underground web. Through these networks, plants grow stronger, absorb more nutrients, store more carbon, and build healthier, moisture-rich soils that resist erosion and support resilient ecosystems.

This installation honours that hidden intelligence. Each fungal form was lovingly crafted by artisans across Kashmir, Jaipur, and Bengaluru, and by a women’s knitting collective in Dehradun. Using recycled newspaper, foraged clay, and surplus fabric, the work mirrors the regenerative spirit of fungi themselves turning what is discarded into new life.

Created by artist Tahir Sultan, whose practice spans cultures and disciplines, The Beauty of Sequestration invites us to look closely, breathe deeply, and learn from the quiet brilliance of the natural world.
