The Last Catch

By Parag Tandel presented by The Heritage Project

0:00
1:42

Parag Tandel’s ‘The Last Catch’ captures the fragile, interdependent relationship between Mumbai’s fishing community, the Kolis, and the ocean that sustains them.

A large prawn, packed with plastic and discarded fishing nets hangs from a crane like a carcass: an offering to the city’s relentless appetite for urbanisation. Tandel uses the installation as a layered metaphor: just as the prawn, an anchor species in the marine ecosystem, is choking with plastic, the Kolis, the original fishing community, are marginalized in a growing metropolis bursting with infrastructure growth.

‘The Last Catch’ acknowledges this interdependence between the land and sea leaving us with a necessary question: What becomes of a city when the last catch holds not life, but the residue of our choices?

This work is commissioned by The Heritage Project & Nature Re, seeded by RPG Foundation. Parag Tandel, a Mumbai-based artist, hailing from the Koli community, takes inspiration from his background to bring out the story of the Kolis.
