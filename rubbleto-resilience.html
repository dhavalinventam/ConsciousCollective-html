Reclaiming cool: from rubble to resilience

Can rubble cool our cities?

You are probably wondering why rubble? And how it can cool our cities, right?

As you stand before this installation, you’re looking at a material that once had only one destination, the landfill. India generates hundreds of millions of tonnes of demolition waste each year, and did you know that less than one percent is recycled.

Through extensive research across brick kilns and demolition sites, the designer - Yashika, a student of NID Ahmedabad- discovered the hidden potential of discarded brick. When crushed into a fine, pozzolanic powder and combined with eco-binders, the discarded brick can transform into a strong, lightweight, low-energy material, one that requires no firing, saves natural resources, and reduces carbon emissions

Drawing inspiration from India’s traditional jaali screens, these modular blocks become more than recycled material; they become a climate solution. Their porous geometry filters light, allows air to flow freely, and lowers indoor temperatures by up to four degrees. The sliding system invites you to adjust shade, create patterns, and experience a living, breathing façade.

Won't it be "cool" to work, live and play in buildings and infrastructure made from this "rubble"? This installation is an invitation to rethink waste? not as the end of a material’s life, but as the beginning of resilience.
