The Living Shore

By Sarang Naik & Lake Heckaman

Welcome to The Living Shore, a 360-degree interactive installation that brings you face-to-face with the warming coastline of Maharashtra. Here, rising sea temperatures are not statistics but shifting tides, altered currents, and fragile lives responding to your presence.

This work blends the photography of Sarang Naik, who has spent years documenting Mumbai’s remarkable shoreline wildlife, with custom software and physics simulations by new media artist, Lake Heckaman.

As you enter, the environment responds to your movement. A small motion can change the water’s flow, alter the city’s outline, or affect the tiny life along the shore. These changes reflect what rising heat is already doing in the real world, weakening corals, lowering oxygen in the sea, and eroding the mangroves that protect our coast.

An underwater soundscape moves through the room like a living pulse, carrying the hidden rhythms of the sea. As the interaction unfolds, joy gives way to awareness.

The Living Shore reveals how every shift, just like every human action, leaves a lasting mark. Consider this an invitation to pause, witness, and remember that our coast is alive, and our choices shape its future.
