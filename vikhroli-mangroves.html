The Vikhroli Mangroves

By Svabhu Kohli

0:00
1:42

Swabhu Kohli's mural brings the Vikhroli mangroves to life, capturing the rich biodiversity that flourishes in this vital urban ecosystem. Through vibrant depictions of species such as the Black-capped Kingfisher, Collared Kingfisher, Painted Stork, Blue Tiger and Crimson butterflies, Fiddler Crabs, Mudskippers, Flamingos, Jackals, Cicadella Viridis, and the Sweet Scented Apple Mangrove flower, the mural celebrates the intricate web of life sustained by these mangroves.

By highlighting these flora and fauna, Kohli emphasizes the ecological importance of mangroves as habitats, nurseries, and buffers that protect both wildlife and the city. The mural is a visual homage to resilience, coexistence, and the often-overlooked natural treasures thriving within Mumbai's urban landscape."

Svabhu Kohli is a visual storyteller whose work blends science, myth, and cultural memory to explore ecological relationships through magical realism. Spanning murals, installations, illustrations, and film, their art has been showcased globally, including at Meta Open Arts and Serendipity Arts Festival.
